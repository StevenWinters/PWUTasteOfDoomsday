Earthquakes Can Be Measured

Earthquakes can be measured Earthquakes can be measured with a device called a seismometer, which detects the seismic waves of the earthquake as a series of zig zags, that data is then used to assign magnitude levels to the earthquake ranging from 1-10. Magnitude being the size of the waves, sometimes also referred to as the amplitude of the waves, generated by the earthquake. Intensity is substituted for magnitude at times, but they are different.

What is Intensity?

Intensity is the amount of damage the earthquake makes to the surrounding area.

A Theory Supporting Another

While earthquakes undoubtedly are a huge effect leading into the separation of continents, especially from Alfred Wegener's Continental Drift Theory; what really is the reason on what caused earthquakes during the times of the Pangea?



Another theory created and implemented to the infamous continental drift is the tectonic plate theory; which tells us that there are divisions of the earth's outer shell. This theory supports the continental drift, describing that long and destructive earthquakes are caused by the sudden shift of the earth's tectonic plates; mainly due to radioactive heat coming from the earth's interior. Therefore causing the once supercontinent to divide and disperse, along with the tectonic plates into smaller ones.



There are 3 types of plate boundaries, namely, convergent, divergent, and transform boundaries. Transform boundaries cause the most frequent and destructive earthquakes due to two plates sliding past each other from the sides. But do not misunderstand, with the eruptions from the volcanoes that were made by the other two boundaries, they also cause earthquakes yet not as strong compared to the transform boundaries.

What To Do and Not To Do

Shaking, violently. In times like these panic rushes through your system. But how do we handle this hazard?

Pre Earthquake:

Earthquakes are unpredictable. You never see them coming. But what can be a solution to be prepared if ever it comes?



WHAT TO DO:

-   Prepare a plan with your family in case it occurs while you're at home.
-   Follow the earthquake plan provided by private and public establishments.
-   Follow the basic steps to protect yourself while the earthquake occurs.
-   In case there is a need of evacuation, you should always have your safety kits ready. Which could include; Radio, batteries, flashlights, whistles, and ready to eat food.

During Earthquake:



WHAT TO DO:

-   Protect your head at all times
  • In times like these, there are tendencies where heave objects, or structures could break and fall.
    • -   Follow the earthquake plan provided by private and public establishments.
  • Everyone is taught this simple routine to do once an earthquake occurs. Also known as the “earthquake drill”
  • Once the ground starts shaking, duck, go under something sturdy to cover, and hold till it stops.
    • -   Stay away from dangerous objects
  • Stay away from objects that could lead you to injuries just like windows, walls, basically things that could break when the occurrence of an earthquake.

    • WHAT NOT TO DO:

    -   Panic
  • Panicking is never a solution.
    • -   Run outside
  • Stay still under a table when the ground is still shaking.
    • -   Underestimate earthquakes
  • If you feel like it's bad, don't just sit still and do nothing about it. Save yourself.

    • Post Earthquake:

    After the earthquake you'll expect aftershocks. Prepare for the worst situation.



    WHAT TO DO:

    -   Check for injuries
  • Once it's safe, check yourself and everyone around you if anyone is hurt or injured.
    • -   Stay alert
  • Listen to news,radio broadcasts, and wait for updates.
